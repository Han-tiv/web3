>  
> 叙事交易，重点是这个代币讲了一个什么样的故事，它能不能引起市场共鸣，结合事件背景、创新性、热度等方面，判断一个代币能走多远。
>  
> 例如$Ani、$Gork，是马斯克旗下新产品催生的叙事，兼具影响力和趣味性，极易传播；$Trump，$Pnut，是重大政治相关事件促成；去年的AI黑客松行情，考究的是Dev履历和业界地位；$Neiro，$Pochita是狗狗币概念的衍生；$Fartcoin，$Useless则强调加密虚无主义。

>  
> 要想抓住这类庄盘，最合适的方法就是地址挖掘，分析是否存在拉盘地址集群以及它们的意图，又或者是通过此前积累的地址库，找到蛛丝马迹。而这种方法的缺点，一是非常耗费时间精力，因为拉盘地址往往是频繁更换的，二是即使被看出了痕迹，一旦底部筹码没有控牢，庄家完全可以选择弃盘，主动权完全在别人手里。
>

> The popular “Wangcai” emoji on WeChat, often referred to as the dog head, comes from a real-life Shiba Inu named Kabosu, born in Japan in November 2005. In 2010, her owner posted a series of photos on a blog. One of them, where Kabosu is lying on a sofa with her paws crossed and a sarcastic look in her eyes, quickly went viral. Netizens turned the image into a meme using broken English phrases like “such wow,” “so skill,” and “very meme.” This gave birth to the iconic Doge meme, which became a pillar of Western internet culture.
>  
> In 2013, users on the forum 4chan used Doge images to flood another subreddit, r/Murica. Unexpectedly, the target audience embraced the meme, leading to its widespread popularity. That same year, Doge was crowned Meme of the Year by KnowYourMeme and inspired the creation of Dogecoin, a cryptocurrency originally intended to parody Bitcoin. After Elon Musk endorsed it multiple times, Dogecoin surged in value and became one of the most successful meme-driven projects ever.
>  
> Soon, the Doge meme entered Chinese internet culture and evolved into local expressions like “gou tou” (dog head), “gou tou bao ming” (dog head to save your life), and eventually “Wangcai.” The word Wangcai sounds identical to “旺财,” which means prosperous wealth in Chinese, giving the emoji an extra layer of lucky symbolism.
>
